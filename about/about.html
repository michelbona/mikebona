About Me
I am Mike Bona, an artist and self-taught photographer from Sandomierz, Poland. Born and raised in the countryside and next to a family-owned junkyard. Kinda because of that I grew up fantasizing about kingdoms made of trash and all things weird.

At an early age I began producing electronic music in Fruity Loops as I loved the 00's club scene with Tiesto's Club Life and David Guetta's F*** Me I'm Famous Radio Shows on top. As I was a very introverted kid I loved to immerse myself in my own imaginary worlds, mainly inspired by video games (Tony Hawk's American Wasteland, Maple Story) and tv cartoons. After secondary school I realized that producing music limits me to only one medium and chains me to the computer and I wanted to let my imagination run free in the real world. My father was a good parent and I had a wonderful childhood, he taught me to work hard and gave me the confidence to be myself and never give up on my ideas. He was a man of steel, not afraid of leading big projects, but emotional enough to support me in my art, which was a very new thing for him in his pragmatic life. So there's me, first artist in the whole family tree, not afraid to stand out and mix two very different worlds. I've always wanted a camera and I got one at the age of 17, a small compact Canon G16, which to this day is my tiny notebook. About that age I got fascinated with instagram and analog photography, I dm'd this nice girl photographer Aleksandra Szajnecka asking for some photography books recommendations. She showed me 'Photography as Contemporary Art' by Charlotte Cotton. I instantly fell in love with the book, the way the artists sacrified their lifes to photograph an idea. Being so inspired I bought my first analog camera Olympus mju ii at an online auction for 400PLN and started photographing my friends. My photoshoot with two girl best friends who looked alike was a small success and got me to the 'Your Works' section in KMAG Magazine. Two years later I bought a Contax G2.

Being raised as a Jehovah Witness and also coming to terms with my sexuality has always caused me confusion and I learned to live a life of constant conflicts. This left an imprint on my art and even though I battled with depression most of the time, I was finding excitement through putting together topics that really don't belong together. I was embracing my weirdness and I found like-minded people who became my friends, for example Hubert Pelc who's my best friend and a stylist. We love to joke about comparing ourselves to Johnny Dufort and Lotta Volkova, the ultimate fashion photography duo.

Speaking of Johnny Dufort, I was very lucky to have won the DoBeDo Mentorship Program and have Johnny as my Mentor for almost two years. He overlooked my first photobook project called 'Family Album', which was recognized by Polish Vogue. In the same year my friend Kuba Brzegowy asked me if I wanted to do a solo exhibition with him as a curator. I agreed and we created a stinky and smelly 'The Fruit Season is Over' exhibition with a big metal tree with rotten apples laying around. That was a lot of fun. I remember picking apples the day before the opening at a random coffee factory.

I recently started an online store to offer high quality original art and limited edition prints directly to fans and collectors that want to buy products straight from the artist. I try to create fun and exciting merch as that's what I like the most about purchasing from my favorite artists. The products on my site are exclusive and I will try to update them regularly with new exciting ideas. My prints are always very nice quality and I use my favorite printing house from Cracow.

I am also ready to produce a photo campaign for your fashion brand. I can provide the best in: models, studio, set designer, make up artist, stylist, movement director, etc. I am based in Poland, but ready to either fly to you or receive a package with pieces from your new collection.
